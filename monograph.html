Imagery in Translation: Monograph on Tree Roots

Roots of Creativity

For my monograph assignment, I created a series of puzzles inspired by the imagery I find in tree roots. Each puzzle is different and varies in difficulty. The puzzle pictured above on the far left combines pairings of drawn and photographic images. Each drawn face is positioned across from the reference photograph. Each photographic image was taken of interesting tree roots I found locally in Baltimore, MD. This puzzle has three drawings paired with three photos.

By contrast, the central puzzle consists of six different drawings. These drawings are coupled with an accordian book that contains the six photographic sources that inspired the ghoulish faces. 75% of the surface of this center puzzle is covered making it medium in difficulty.

The last puzzle, pictured on the far right, is perhaps the most difficult to solve because all surfaces are covered in imagery. The surface design was created by collaging photographic images of tree roots together and then drawing digitally over the natural infrastructure.
